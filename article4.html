Family, Eternity, and Memory 🌅

Today is a day rich in meaning for me and for the history of my family. I would like to capture it by sharing an experience that occurred a few days ago. 👇

I was with the children. 👧👶 My husband was away for a few days for work. When it was time to take the dog out, I realized that the kids had no desire to come with me. ❌

After a nice moment of play among them, I gradually stopped them, without causing net interruptions in their complicity. 🏃‍♀️🚶‍♀️🧎‍♀️ The eldest immediately accepted my invitation to sit in a circle on the floor. The youngest took a little longer: he didn't want to give up the game in which he was so into the flow. Then he agreed and sat down to complete our little circle. ☯️

I asked them to hold their hands. 🤝 Not loosely, but to grip each other as if making a commitment, with the promise passing through those hands. 🔥 We laughed and then we got serious. I asked them to look into each other's eyes. 👥

🗣 "Mommy is leaving now and won't be here for a little while. When Mommy and Daddy are not here, we are our family. Maja for Joel. 👧 Joel for Maja. 👶"

At the end, the eldest lunged at the youngest for a hug of the kind that almost bites. Joel let himself be caught, grabbing onto whatever he could of his big sister. ✨ With teary eyes, I hugged them. 🥹

The whole thing lasted maybe 5 minutes. Time, when it's full, is eternal. 🌅 This memory will live in me forever. 🙏

It's the eternity I celebrate in its sadness on this day when three years ago, my mom passed away. A sadness that reminds me of this hug, which like many others, surely more than others, I carry with me beyond time. 💜
